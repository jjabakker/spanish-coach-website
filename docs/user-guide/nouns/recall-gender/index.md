# Recall Noun Gender

The Recall Gender drill is similar to [Pick the Correct Article](../correct-article/), but with the article-prompt removed. You see the **noun alone** — no *el*, no *la* — and recall whether it is masculine or feminine.

---

## How it works

1. A Spanish noun appears with **no article**.
2. Tap **masculine** or **feminine** (presented as *el* and *la* buttons in the app, or as gender labels — the exact layout depends on your settings).
3. The correct gender is revealed, with the full *el* / *la* form shown for reinforcement.

Each noun appears once per session. Your answers feed the adaptive engine.

---

## Why a separate "no article" drill?

In real reading and listening, you often encounter Spanish nouns **without** a preceding article — in lists, headlines, captions, vocabulary apps, dictionaries, or after possessives (*mi casa*, *su carro*) where the gender is hidden. The article-test version of this drill gives you the article-shape; this version forces you to recall gender from the noun itself.

It's a slightly harder version of the same skill, and is most useful once [Pick the Correct Article](../correct-article/) feels comfortable.

---

## Tips

- Run this drill after [Meaning Test](../meaning-test/) and [Pick the Correct Article](../correct-article/) feel comfortable.
- If you're consistently getting consonant-ending nouns wrong, return to the [Noun Gender Rules](../noun-gender/) study screen for the patterns.
- This is also a useful drill before tackling adjective agreement, since adjective forms depend on noun gender — getting gender automatic makes adjective agreement easier downstream.
