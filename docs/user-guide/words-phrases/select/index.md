# Select Words and Phrases

Spanish Coach includes around 540 Words & Phrases entries — adjectives, adverbs, prepositions, numbers, greetings, time/date expressions, and common everyday phrases. The Select screen lets you narrow these down to the set you want to practise.

---

## Filters

- **CEFR Level** — A1 (Beginner) through C2 (Mastery).
- **Frequency** — Very Common, Common, or Less Common.
- **Topic** — limit to one or more categories such as greetings, food, travel, numbers, time, body, work, …

The screen always shows how many entries match your current filters. Tap **Matching words** to browse the full list before confirming.

---

## What's actually in here?

This section is a mixed bag of everything that isn't a verb or a noun:

- **Adjectives** — masculine and feminine forms are merged into one entry. The flashcard shows the form appropriate to the context.
- **Adverbs** — *siempre*, *nunca*, *muy*, *bien*, *mal*, *despacio*, …
- **Prepositions and conjunctions** — *para*, *por*, *aunque*, *sin embargo*, *a pesar de*, …
- **Numbers** — cardinal numbers, ordinals, fractions.
- **Greetings and social phrases** — *buenos días*, *mucho gusto*, *encantado* / *encantada*, *de nada*, …
- **Time and date expressions** — days, months, seasons, *en punto*, *hace falta*, *de vez en cuando*, …
- **Everyday phrases** — useful chunks you'd want to produce in a real conversation.

---

## Word pool vs session size

These are two separate things:

- Your **word pool** is the set of entries eligible to appear in any session — the result of your filters.
- Your **session size** is how many of those entries appear in a single sitting (10, 20, 30, 50, or All).

Each session, the app picks the best N entries from your pool, prioritising unseen and weak words.

---

## Tips

- Topic filtering is especially useful here. Going on a trip? Restrict to **Travel + Greetings** for a focused phrase pool.
- The Words & Phrases section is best used **alongside** Verbs and Nouns rather than in place of them. It picks up the connective tissue (adverbs, prepositions, common phrases) that the structured Verbs and Nouns sections don't cover.
